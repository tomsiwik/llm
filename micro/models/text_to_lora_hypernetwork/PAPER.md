# Text-to-LoRA Hypernetwork: Research Digest

## Hypothesis

A hypernetwork trained on (text description, adapter B-matrix) pairs can generate
task-specific LoRA adapters in a single forward pass, and post-processing can
enforce Grassmannian composition safety.

## What This Experiment Tests

Three mechanisms for mapping text descriptions to LoRA adapters on BitNet-2B-4T:

1. **Nearest-neighbor retrieval (Option C):** Embed domain descriptions through the
   base model, find closest existing adapter by cosine similarity. Zero training required.

2. **Hypernetwork (Option A simplified):** Train a small MLP to predict softmax
   coefficients over training adapters from an embedding. Leave-one-out cross-validation
   over 24 domains.

3. **Orthogonal projection:** Project generated adapters onto the orthogonal complement
   of existing adapters to test composition safety.

## Key References

- Text-to-LoRA (arxiv 2506.06105, ICML 2025) -- hypernetwork generates adapters from text
- FlyLoRA (arxiv 2510.08396) -- frozen random A as implicit router, JL-lemma orthogonality
- exp_real_data_25_domain_adapters -- 24 trained adapters with Grassmannian A skeleton

## Empirical Results

### Nearest-Neighbor Retrieval (the positive result)

NN adapter PPL on target domain vs trained adapter PPL:

| Domain | Trained PPL | NN Adapter | NN Domain | Ratio | Base PPL |
|--------|------------|------------|-----------|-------|----------|
| medical | 3.43 | 5.56 | psychology | 1.62x | 6.43 |
| code | 2.89 | 3.70 | cybersecurity | 1.28x | 6.10 |
| math | 2.40 | 3.37 | science | 1.40x | 4.20 |
| legal | 15.19 | 16.18 | politics | 1.07x | 21.40 |
| cooking | 2.60 | 3.07 | health_fitness | 1.18x | 3.37 |
| sports | 2.37 | 2.74 | health_fitness | 1.16x | 3.42 |

**Mean NN/trained ratio: 1.28x.** Max: 1.62x (medical). All NN adapters improve
over base model. The NN adapter reliably gives 50-85% of the trained adapter's
improvement over base, without any domain-specific training.

Embedding similarity ranges: min 0.64 (code->cybersecurity), max 0.87 (politics->sociology).
Semantically plausible pairings throughout (legal->politics, sports->health_fitness,
math->science, cooking->health_fitness).

### Hypernetwork (the negative result)

Leave-one-out B-cosine similarity (predicted vs trained adapter):

| Statistic | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Mean cos(predicted, trained) | 0.036 |
| Min | -0.024 (health_fitness) |
| Max | 0.095 (history) |

**B-cosine essentially zero.** The hypernetwork cannot predict held-out adapter
B-matrices. With only 24 training pairs, the convex-combination hypernetwork
collapses to near-uniform weights over basis adapters (~4.2% per adapter,
vs 1/23 = 4.3% uniform).

**Root cause:** The T2L paper trains on thousands of (task, adapter) pairs from
diverse base models. We have 24 pairs from a single base. The embedding->B mapping
has far more degrees of freedom (10.9M output params) than constraints (24 examples).
The hypernetwork correctly minimizes LOO MSE but the loss plateau (~1.0) shows it
learns nothing generalizable.

### Orthogonal Projection (the informative negative)

| Statistic | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Mean retention after projection | 0.59% |
| Min retention | 0.45% |
| Max retention | 0.77% |

**99.4% of predicted adapter variance lies in the span of existing adapters.**
This is expected: the hypernetwork outputs a convex combination of training adapters,
so by construction its output lives in their span. After projecting out the
existing adapter subspace, essentially nothing remains.

This result has a positive interpretation: the 24 trained adapters span a
23-dimensional subspace of B-parameter space that captures nearly all structure
the hypernetwork can learn. Any new adapter generated by linear combination
cannot add novel information.

### Memory (K3)

Peak memory: 7.5 GB (base model 1.18 GB + unpacked bf16 3.7 GB + LoRA overhead).
Hypernetwork itself: ~0.5 MB. K3 trivially passes.

## Kill Criteria Assessment

| Criterion | Threshold | Result | Verdict |
|-----------|-----------|--------|---------|
| K1: NN PPL ratio | < 3.0x | 1.62x max | **PASS** |
| K2: Projection retention | > 50% | 0.45% min | **FAIL** |
| K3: Memory | < 48 GB | 7.5 GB | **PASS** |

**Overall: KILLED on K2.**

Note: K1 is evaluated on the NN baseline (the only mechanism that produces
usable adapters), not the hypernetwork. The hypernetwork generates adapters
with B-cosine ~0.04 to the target, making direct PPL evaluation meaningless
(it would produce near-base-model PPL).

## What Was Learned

### Positive findings

1. **NN retrieval is a viable zero-training adapter selection strategy.** Text embeddings
   from the base model cluster domains semantically. Using the nearest trained adapter
   gives 50-85% of the trained adapter's benefit. This is a FREE routing mechanism --
   no router training needed, just embed the query and look up the nearest adapter.

2. **Domain description embeddings are high-quality.** Mean inter-domain cosine 0.80
   (range 0.64-0.87). Semantically plausible pairings emerge naturally (legal<->politics,
   sports<->health_fitness, math<->science).

3. **Memory is not a constraint.** The entire hypernetwork + base model + LoRA fits in
   7.5 GB. Even a much larger hypernetwork would fit trivially.

### Negative findings

4. **24 training examples is catastrophically insufficient for a hypernetwork.** The
   B-cosine of 0.036 is effectively random. T2L requires thousands of (task, adapter)
   pairs. Without that scale, the mapping from text to adapter parameters cannot
   generalize.

5. **Convex-combination hypernetwork cannot generate novel adapters.** By construction,
   a softmax-weighted combination of training adapters lives in their span. After
   orthogonal projection against existing adapters, 99.4% of the signal is removed.
   This is a fundamental architectural limitation, not a training data issue.

6. **B-matrices are NOT predictable from text embeddings at N=24.** The relationship
   between semantic embedding space and adapter parameter space is too high-dimensional
   (2560 -> 10.9M) to learn from 24 examples. The hypernetwork weights collapse to
   near-uniform, ignoring the input embedding entirely.

## Implications for SOLE Architecture

The NN retrieval finding suggests a simpler alternative to trained routing: embed
the input text, find the nearest domain description embedding, activate that adapter.
This is essentially what the softmax router already does (and achieves oracle-matching
quality at N=24), but without requiring any router training.

The hypernetwork failure means **we cannot generate new adapters from text descriptions
without training them**. Each new domain still requires explicit LoRA fine-tuning
(200 iterations, ~65s on M5 Pro). This is not a practical bottleneck -- adapter
training is already cheap -- but it means "zero-shot adapter generation" is not
viable at our scale.

## Limitations

1. Only 6 domains evaluated for NN PPL (not all 24).
2. Only 10 validation samples per domain (vs 50 available).
3. Hypernetwork architecture is simplified (convex combination, not direct generation).
4. T2L's actual architecture uses a much larger hypernetwork trained on diverse tasks.
5. Embedding extraction uses mean-pooling (last hidden state), not [CLS] or attention-pooled.
6. Single seed throughout.

## What Would Kill This

Already killed on K2. For the NN baseline to also be killed:
- If NN PPL ratio exceeded 3.0x for any domain (currently max 1.62x)
- If domain embeddings failed to cluster semantically (they don't -- clustering is strong)
