# MATH.md: M2P Composition at N=5 with Grassmannian Guarantee

## A. Failure Mode Identification

**Disease (not symptom):** When N independently-trained domain adapters are additively
composed as W_composed = W_base + Σ_{i=1}^N Δ_i, parameter interference corrupts each
adapter's domain knowledge. The degenerate state is that the composed model outputs
a mixture of all domains rather than the requested domain — quality collapses from
single-adapter level toward the all-domain centroid.

**Root cause analysis:** Two distinct interference channels exist:
1. **Parameter-space interference:** ⟨Δ_i, Δ_j⟩_F ≠ 0 causes gradient cross-talk
   during fine-tuning AND algebraic cancellation at inference. This is the ROOT CAUSE.
2. **Activation-space interference:** Even with parameter orthogonality, B_i(A_i·x)
   and B_j(A_j·x) write to the same output dimension. This is a SYMPTOM of operating
   in a shared output space; empirically rare when adapters are trained on distinct
   domains (Finding #3: cos=0.0002).

**This experiment isolates:** Is parameter-space interference eliminated by Grassmannian
A-slots? Can a per-token router then select the correct adapter among 5 composed experts?

The prior failures (#341, #342, #343) all involve a DIFFERENT disease: M2P bottleneck
preventing context-dependent B-matrix generation under multi-domain joint training.
This experiment avoids that disease entirely by training each M2P independently on a
single domain.

## B. Prior Mathematical Foundations

### B.1 Grassmannian Packing and QR Orthogonality

**Theorem (Gram-Schmidt / QR):** For any n×k matrix X with n ≥ k·N, the QR
decomposition X = QR produces Q ∈ ℝ^{n×k·N} with orthonormal columns:
Q^T Q = I_{k·N}.

Partitioning Q into N blocks of k columns each: Q = [Q_1 | Q_2 | ... | Q_N],
we have Q_i^T Q_j = 0 for all i ≠ j, and Q_i^T Q_i = I_k.

*Reference:* Golub & Van Loan, "Matrix Computations" (4th ed.), Ch. 5.

**Capacity condition:** At d=256, rank=4, N=5: need 5×4=20 ≤ 256. Satisfied with margin 12.8×.

### B.2 Frobenius Inner Product of LoRA Adapters

For LoRA adapter Δ_i = B_i A_i^T (where A_i ∈ ℝ^{d×r}, B_i ∈ ℝ^{r×d_out}):

⟨Δ_i, Δ_j⟩_F = trace(Δ_i^T Δ_j)
              = trace((B_i A_i^T)^T (B_j A_j^T))
              = trace(A_i B_i^T B_j A_j^T)
              = trace(B_j A_j^T A_i B_i^T)   [cyclic permutation of trace]

This equals zero if and only if A_j^T A_i = 0.

*Reference:* Horn & Johnson, "Matrix Analysis" (2nd ed.), §0.2 (trace identities).

### B.3 Linear Separability of Domain Representations

**Theorem (Cover 1965, "Geometrical and statistical properties of systems of linear
inequalities"):** For N classes of data points in ℝ^d, if the hidden-state
representations are linearly separable, a linear classifier achieves 100% accuracy.
If not perfectly separable, a linear probe achieves accuracy proportional to the
margin.

**Finding #310:** Per-token hidden states are linearly separable at 98.3% accuracy
with a single-pass MLP router. This provides the empirical grounding that domain
representations ARE linearly separable in the toy GPT's hidden space.

### B.4 LoRA Additivity under Orthogonal Subspaces

**Theorem (Naive LoRA Summation, arXiv:2508.11985, Theorem 1):** When adapters have
orthogonal row spaces (A_i^T A_j = 0), the composed model W + Σ_i Δ_i preserves the
individual adapter properties: for any input x in the subspace of adapter i, the output
of the composed model equals the output of the single-adapter model.

*Formal condition:* A_i^T A_j = 0 ∀ i≠j ⟹ Δ_i^T Δ_j = 0_F (from §B.2).

## C. Proof of Guarantee

### Theorem 1 (Parameter Orthogonality under Grassmannian A)

**Theorem 1.** Let A_1, ..., A_N ∈ ℝ^{d×r} be the A-matrices of N LoRA adapters,
generated by partitioning the columns of an orthogonal matrix Q ∈ ℝ^{d×Nr}
(via QR decomposition of a random d×Nr Gaussian matrix). Let B_1, ..., B_N ∈ ℝ^{r×d_out}
be arbitrary B-matrices (trained by any method, including independent M2P).

Then:

    ⟨Δ_i, Δ_j⟩_F = trace(A_i B_i^T B_j A_j^T) = 0   for all i ≠ j

*Proof.*

Step 1 (QR construction): By the QR algorithm, Q has orthonormal columns.
Partitioning Q = [A_1 | ... | A_N] with A_k = Q[:, (k-1)r:kr], we have by definition:

    A_i^T A_j = (Q[:,(i-1)r:ir])^T (Q[:,(j-1)r:jr])
              = (Q^T Q)[(i-1)r:ir, (j-1)r:jr]
              = I_{Nr}[(i-1)r:ir, (j-1)r:jr]
              = 0_{r×r}   since the row/column index ranges are disjoint.

Step 2 (Adapter inner product): By the Frobenius inner product formula:

    ⟨Δ_i, Δ_j⟩_F = trace(A_i B_i^T B_j A_j^T)

Let M = B_i^T B_j ∈ ℝ^{r×r}. Then:

    trace(A_i M A_j^T) = trace(A_j^T A_i M)   [cyclic permutation of trace]
                       = trace(0_{r×r} · M)    [Step 1]
                       = 0.

Step 3 (Independence from B): The above holds for ANY B_i, B_j ∈ ℝ^{r×d_out}.
In particular, it holds when B_i and B_j are generated by independent M2P transformers,
SFT training, or random initialization.

QED.

**Corollary 1.** The composed adapter Δ_sum = Σ_i Δ_i satisfies:

    ‖Δ_sum‖_F^2 = Σ_i ‖Δ_i‖_F^2

i.e., the composed adapter's energy is the sum of individual energies (no cancellation
and no amplification). This follows directly from the Pythagorean theorem in Frobenius
space under mutual orthogonality.

### Theorem 2 (Routing Correctness — Distinguishability of Domain Adapters)

**Theorem 2.** Let x_i be an input token from domain i, and let φ: ℝ^d → ℝ^d be
the base model's hidden state mapping. If the hidden state representations
φ(x_1), ..., φ(x_N) are linearly separable (Finding #310: 98.3% accuracy), then
a linear router r: ℝ^d → Δ_N (N-class softmax) achieves routing accuracy ≥ 98.3%.

*Proof sketch.* Linear separability of {φ(x_i)} means there exists W_r and threshold
t_i such that argmax_j W_r φ(x_i)_j = i for at least 98.3% of tokens.
This is the definition of linear separability from Finding #310.
The softmax router r(x) = softmax(W_r φ(x)) converges to this linear classifier
during training on labeled domain data.

**Important constraint:** This theorem applies when the router has access to
domain-labeled training data, OR when input tokens are inherently domain-distinct
(domain tokens are written with distinct vocabulary/structure). In this experiment,
the 5 domains (arithmetic, sort, parity, reverse, repeat) use structurally distinct
token patterns (+, >, *, bit strings) that provide strong domain signals.

QED.

---

### Theorem 3 (Composition Quality Bound)

**Theorem 3.** Under Grassmannian A (Theorem 1), the composed model output for
domain-i input is:

    y_composed(x_i) = W_base x_i + Σ_j r_j(x_i) · Δ_j x_i

where r_j(x_i) ∈ [0,1] is the routing weight for adapter j given input x_i.

If routing is accurate (r_i(x_i) ≈ 1, r_j(x_i) ≈ 0 for j≠i), then:

    y_composed(x_i) ≈ W_base x_i + Δ_i x_i = y_single_adapter_i(x_i)

The quality degradation from composition vs single adapter is:

    Δ_quality ≤ Σ_{j≠i} r_j(x_i) · ‖Δ_j‖ · ‖x_i‖

This is bounded by the routing error times the norms of non-target adapters.

**Prediction:** If routing accuracy ≥ 80% (K852 threshold), composition quality
should be within ~20% of single-adapter quality (since the 20% misrouted tokens
contribute at most ‖Δ_j‖·‖x_i‖ perturbation, which is small when adapters have
controlled scale).

QED.

## E. Assumptions and Breaking Conditions

| Assumption | Consequence if Violated | Kill Signal |
|------------|------------------------|-------------|
| A1: d ≥ N·r (capacity) | QR cannot produce N orthogonal A-matrices | Hard crash — assertion failure |
| A2: QR produces orthonormal columns (float32) | Numerical error in A_i^T A_j | |cos_max| > 1e-5 |
| A3: Domain inputs are structurally distinct | Router cannot distinguish domains | K852 FAIL: routing <50% |
| A4: Independent M2P training produces non-trivial B | B-matrices trivially zero | All qualities ≈ 0% |
| A5: Activation-space interference is negligible | Theorem 3 bound loose | Composition PPL much worse than predicted |

**Critical:** Assumption A5 is NOT guaranteed by Theorem 1. Theorem 1 guarantees
PARAMETER-space orthogonality only. If B_i A_i x and B_j A_j x point in opposite
directions in the output space, activation interference can still occur even though
the adapters are parameter-orthogonal. Finding #3 (cos=0.0002) shows this is
empirically rare but not provably zero.

## F. Worked Example (d=8, r=2, N=2)

Let d=8, r=2, N=2 (so N·r=4 ≤ d=8).

**Step 1: Generate A matrices via QR.**

Sample X ∈ ℝ^{8×4} ~ N(0,1):
```
X = [[0.5, 0.3, -0.2, 0.1],
     [0.1, -0.4, 0.8, 0.2],
     ...  (8 rows total)]
```

QR decomposition: X = Q R where Q ∈ ℝ^{8×4}, Q^T Q = I_4.

Partition: A_1 = Q[:, 0:2], A_2 = Q[:, 2:4].

**Step 2: Verify orthogonality.**

A_1^T A_2 = (Q[:, 0:2])^T (Q[:, 2:4]) = (Q^T Q)[0:2, 2:4] = I_4[0:2, 2:4] = 0_{2×2}.

**Step 3: Choose arbitrary B matrices.**

B_1 = [[1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8],    (r=2, d_out=8)
        [9, 8, 7, 6, 5, 4, 3, 2]]

B_2 = [[0.5, -1, 2, 0, 1, -2, 3, 0],
        [1, 1, -1, -1, 2, 2, -2, -2]]

**Step 4: Compute Frobenius inner product.**

Δ_1 = B_1 A_1^T ∈ ℝ^{8×8}
Δ_2 = B_2 A_2^T ∈ ℝ^{8×8}

⟨Δ_1, Δ_2⟩_F = trace(Δ_1^T Δ_2)
              = trace(A_1 B_1^T B_2 A_2^T)
              = trace(B_2 A_2^T A_1 B_1^T)   [cyclic]
              = trace(B_2 · 0 · B_1^T)        [A_2^T A_1 = 0]
              = 0.   QED (calculable by hand for any B_1, B_2).

**Capacity check at experiment scale:** d=256, r=4, N=5 → 5×4=20 ≤ 256. Capacity margin: 12.8×.

## G. Complexity and Architecture Connection

### FLOPs

| Component | FLOPs |
|-----------|-------|
| Grassmannian A generation (once) | O(d·(Nr)^2) = O(256 × 400) ≈ 100K |
| M2P forward (per domain, 1 token) | O(L·T·d·M2P_hidden) ≈ O(4 × 32 × 64 × 64) = 525K |
| LoRA forward (per token, N adapters) | O(N·r·d) = O(5 × 4 × 256) = 5K per module |
| Router forward (per token) | O(N·d) = O(5 × 256) = 1.3K |

### Memory

| Component | Memory |
|-----------|--------|
| Grassmannian A matrices | N·L·M · d·r · 4B = 5×4×5×256×4×4B = 1.6 MB |
| M2P parameters | ~300K params × 4B = 1.2 MB |
| Per-adapter B matrices (saved to disk) | N·L·M · r·d_out × 4B ≈ 0.5 MB |

### Architecture connection

The GrassmannianLoRA class implements: output = W_base(x) + scale · (x @ A) @ B
where A is frozen (d×r), B is trained (r×d_out). This matches the LoRA formula with:
- Left factor: A^T (d×r matrix applied to input)
- Right factor: B (r×d_out output projection)

Note: in this codebase, A is stored as (d, r) so x @ A gives (T, r), then @ B gives (T, d_out).
This is the transpose of the standard LoRA convention but mathematically equivalent.

## Scope Statement (Critical)

**What is guaranteed (Theorem 1):** Parameter-space orthogonality is PROVEN.
⟨Δ_i, Δ_j⟩_F = 0 for all i≠j, regardless of B content, regardless of training dynamics.
This is a mathematical identity derivable from QR properties.

**What is estimated (empirical):** Activation-space interference is NOT zero in general.
Finding #3 (cos=0.0002) provides strong empirical evidence it is negligible in practice,
but there is no theorem bounding activation-space interference to zero.

**What this experiment does NOT test:**
- Multi-domain joint M2P training (that's the killed experiments)
- Promotion to base model
- Macro-scale performance at d=3584 (Qwen3-4B)
- Continual learning (adapters trained sequentially vs independently)

## Self-Test (MANDATORY)

**1. What is the ONE mathematical property that makes the failure mode impossible?**

QR decomposition produces orthonormal columns: Q^T Q = I. This makes A_i^T A_j = 0
for disjoint column partitions, which makes ⟨Δ_i, Δ_j⟩_F = 0 for ANY B_i, B_j.
Parameter-space interference is geometrically impossible under this constraint.

**2. Which existing theorem(s) does the proof build on?**

- QR/Gram-Schmidt orthogonality (Golub & Van Loan, Matrix Computations, Ch. 5)
- Frobenius trace identity: trace(ABC) = trace(CAB) (Horn & Johnson, Matrix Analysis)
- Naive LoRA Summation (arXiv:2508.11985, Theorem 1): orthogonal row spaces → adapter additivity
- Linear separability → linear routing (Cover 1965)

**3. What specific numbers does the proof predict?**

- P1: |cos| = 0.000000 (float32 zero, Theorem 1 is exact)
- P2: Composition PPL ratio ≤ 1.20 (Theorem 3 + 80% routing assumption)
- P3: Routing accuracy ≥ 80% (Theorem 2 + Finding #310 98.3% baseline)
- P4: General quality degradation < 10pp (K851 kill criterion)

**4. What would FALSIFY the proof (not just the experiment)?**

The proof is wrong if: A_i^T A_j ≠ 0 despite QR decomposition. This would require
the QR algorithm to fail to produce orthonormal columns — impossible in exact arithmetic,
but possible if float32 numerical errors are large enough. Check: |cos| > 1e-5 would
indicate numerical failure of the QR guarantee.

The routing theorem is wrong if: linearly separable representations (Finding #310)
do NOT translate to learnable routing in composition context. This is possible if
the composed model's hidden states no longer have the same separability as the
single-model hidden states. Empirical check via K852.

**5. How many hyperparameters does this approach add?**

Hyperparameters: LoRA rank r=4, scale=2.0, M2P hidden dim=64, M2P steps=500 per domain.
That is 4 hyperparameters. Why can't they be derived?
- r=4: governed by capacity constraint (N·r ≤ d). For N=5, d=256, any r ≤ 51 works.
  r=4 chosen from prior experiments for parameter efficiency.
- scale=2.0: empirically safe from prior experiments (scale=5 at real scale, scale=2 at toy).
  Theorem 3 bound depends on scale but doesn't uniquely determine it.
- M2P hidden dim=64: architectural choice for parameter budget.
- M2P steps=500: convergence; not derivable from theory (depends on optimization landscape).

**6. Hack check: Am I adding fix #N to an existing stack?**

No. This experiment does NOT add fixes to the failed multi-domain M2P stack (#341, #342, #343).
It avoids the multi-domain bottleneck entirely by training each M2P independently.
The approach is: train N separate single-domain M2Ps (each proven to work from Finding #339),
then verify that Theorem 1 guarantees their composition is interference-free.
This is a VERIFICATION experiment, not a fix. The single mechanism is Grassmannian A.
