# MATH.md: M2P Macro Quality — n_train≥T Guarantee at 2× d_model

**Experiment type:** Guided exploration (Type 2)
**Prior finding:** exp_m2p_data_scale (Finding #359, supported): at d_model=256, n=2000 +
  GL early stopping achieves 97.6% of SFT quality. The n_train≥T structural guarantee
  plus GL early stopping is the validated recipe.
**Proven framework:** Ghadimi-Lan O(1/T) convergence + Bartlett et al. benign overfitting
  threshold + Prechelt GL early stopping.
**Unknown being discovered:** Does the same fixed recipe (L=2, d_M2P=64, n=2000, GL) achieve
  ≥85% of SFT when the only change is d_model: 256 → 512?

---

## A. Failure Mode Identification

**Disease (risk, not hypothesized):** At d_model=512, the B-matrix target for each
module is larger. The M2P's output heads must generate B ∈ R^{rank × d_out} where
d_out ∈ {512, 512, 512, 512, 2048} (vs {256, 256, 256, 256, 1024} at micro).
The total target dimensionality doubles.

If the M2P bottleneck (d_M2P=64, N_MEMORY=32) is insufficient for the larger target,
the training loss cannot descend far enough to achieve high quality_ratio. This is a
capacity failure, not an overfitting failure.

**Two distinct failure modes:**
1. **Capacity bottleneck** (K884): The M2P hidden dimension is too small to represent
   B-matrices for 512-dim modules. Signal: quality_ratio < 60%.
2. **Overfitting** (K883): Even with n≥T satisfied, the 512-dim targets are harder to
   generalize from n=2000 samples. Signal: train-val gap ≥ 0.7 nats.

**Is this the root cause or a symptom?** This is a frontier extension (Type 2): we have a
proven recipe at d=256 and want to know if it scales to d=512. We are not diagnosing a
failure yet — we are testing whether the guarantee transfers.

## B. Prior Mathematical Foundations

### B.1 Ghadimi-Lan O(1/T) and the n_train≥T Structural Guarantee

**Theorem 2.1 (Ghadimi & Lan, arXiv:1309.5549):**
For L-smooth non-convex function f, SGD satisfies:

    min_{t=0,...,T-1} E[||∇f(x_t)||²] ≤ (2L(f(x₀) - f*)) / T + σ²/(bT)

**Critical assumption:** Unbiased gradient estimates with bounded variance.

**n_train≥T structural guarantee (proven in Finding #359 / MATH.md m2p_data_scale Theorem 1):**
When n_train ≥ T (each sample seen at most once), the gradient estimator satisfies the
Ghadimi-Lan i.i.d. assumption, and the Hardt et al. (2016) generalization bound gives:

    E[L_gen(θ_T)] - L_train(θ_T) ≤ O(T/n_train) ≤ O(1)  (bounded constant)

This is a sufficient condition. It does NOT depend on d_model — the condition is on the
data/compute ratio, not on the model dimensions.

**Corollary (n* at T=1000, 80/20 split):**
    n* = T / 0.8 = 1000 / 0.8 = 1250 samples.
At n=2000 (n_train=1600): T/n_train = 0.625 epochs. No cycling. Structural guarantee holds.
This derivation is INDEPENDENT of d_model.

### B.2 Benign Overfitting and d_eff Scaling (Bartlett et al., arXiv:1906.11300)

**Theorem (Bartlett et al., 2020):**
For linear regression with n samples and model effective dimensionality d_eff, overfitting
is benign (does not degrade generalization) only when:

    n ≥ d_eff

**Application to M2P B-matrix targets:**
The M2P generates B-matrices {B_li_mi} with total dimension:

    Micro (d=256): N_LAYERS × N_MODULES × LORA_RANK × max(d_out)
      = 2 × 5 × 4 × max(256, 256, 256, 256, 1024) = 2 × 5 × 4 × 1024 = 40,960
    
    Macro (d=512): N_LAYERS × N_MODULES × LORA_RANK × max(d_out)
      = 2 × 5 × 4 × max(512, 512, 512, 512, 2048) = 2 × 5 × 4 × 2048 = 81,920

**d_eff estimate using Bartlett's framework:**
The B-matrices are generated by the M2P from hidden states. The effective rank of the
mapping M2P: H → {B} depends on the intrinsic dimensionality of the B-matrix targets.
As d_model doubles, the B-matrix targets contain twice as many parameters.

By Bartlett's framework, the effective dimensionality d_eff scales with the target
parameter count. Specifically:

    Micro: d_eff ≈ rank(B_target space) × total_params ≈ O(1024)
    Macro: d_eff ≈ 2 × d_eff_micro ≈ O(2048)

This is an ESTIMATE, not a sharp bound. The actual d_eff depends on the structure of
B-matrices that the M2P must learn. Key question: does n=2000 still satisfy n >> d_eff
at d_model=512?

**Comparison:**
- Micro: n=2000 vs d_eff≈1024 → n/d_eff ≈ 2 (marginal but sufficient — confirmed by Finding #359)
- Macro: n=2000 vs d_eff≈2048 → n/d_eff ≈ 1 (at the boundary — might still be benign)

Bartlett's theorem predicts that at n/d_eff ≈ 1, overfitting becomes more pronounced.
This is the quantitative reason to expect some degradation vs the micro baseline (97.6%),
but NOT catastrophic failure (K884: <60%).

**Prediction from Bartlett:** At d=512 with n=2000, overfitting is marginally more than
at d=256. Quality floor: ≥85% of SFT (allowing ~12pp degradation vs micro's 97.6%).

### B.3 GL Early Stopping as Infrastructure (Prechelt, 1998)

**Prechelt (1998), "Early Stopping — But When?":**

    GL(t) = 100 × (val_loss(t) / min_{s≤t} val_loss(s) − 1)

Stop when GL(t) > α = 5.0 for PATIENCE = 5 consecutive checks.

This is infrastructure, not a hyperparameter choice. It prevents runaway overfitting
independent of d_model by monitoring the ratio of current to best validation loss.
Its effectiveness does NOT depend on d_model.

**Finding #359 quantitative result:** GL early stopping contributed +7.6pp at d=256.
At d=512, similar or greater benefit is expected if overfitting is the limiting factor.

### B.4 M2P Capacity at d_M2P=64 (Findings #355, #357)

**Finding #355 (exp_m2p_bottleneck_width, supported):** d_M2P=64 is sufficient at d=256.
**Finding #357 (exp_m2p_depth, supported):** L=2 saturates at d=256.

IMPORTANT: These findings were established at d_model=256. They do NOT guarantee
sufficiency at d_model=512. This experiment tests whether the same fixed M2P architecture
remains sufficient when the BASE model doubles in size.

The output heads of M2P are:
    out_heads[mname]: d_M2P=64 → N_LAYERS × LORA_RANK × d_out

At d=256: d_out ∈ {256, 256, 256, 256, 1024} → max output = 2 × 4 × 1024 = 8,192
At d=512: d_out ∈ {512, 512, 512, 512, 2048} → max output = 2 × 4 × 2048 = 16,384

The output head for fc1 must project from d_M2P=64 to 16,384 parameters at d=512
(vs 8,192 at d=256). This is a 2× increase in output dimensionality per head.
Whether the bottleneck at d_M2P=64 is sufficient for this task is the core question.

## C. Proof of Guarantee

### Theorem 1 (n_train≥T Structural Guarantee is d_model-independent)

**Theorem 1.** The n_train≥T structural guarantee derived in exp_m2p_data_scale
(Ghadimi-Lan + Hardt et al.) is independent of d_model. At n=2000 (n_train=1600)
and T=1000, the i.i.d. gradient sampling condition is satisfied regardless of
whether d_model=256 or d_model=512.

*Proof.*
The Ghadimi-Lan theorem (arXiv:1309.5549, Theorem 2.1) requires:
    (a) L-smooth loss function f
    (b) Unbiased gradient estimates: E[g_t] = ∇f(θ_t)
    (c) Bounded gradient noise: E[||g_t - ∇f(θ_t)||²] ≤ σ²

Condition (b) holds when at step t, the sample x_t is drawn independently from
the training distribution. This is guaranteed when n_train ≥ T (each sample
appears at most once in T steps of single-pass SGD).

The condition n_train ≥ T involves ONLY the data size n_train and the number of
gradient steps T. It does NOT involve d_model, d_M2P, or any architectural
dimension. The transformer architecture affects the constant L (smoothness of loss)
and σ² (gradient noise magnitude) but NOT whether the i.i.d. condition holds.

Therefore:
    n=2000 (n_train=1600), T=1000 → T/n_train = 0.625 < 1 epoch
    → i.i.d. sampling guaranteed
    → Ghadimi-Lan O(1/T) bound applies to training loss
    → Hardt et al. generalization bound: E[L_gen - L_train] ≤ O(T/n_train) = O(0.625)

This holds at d_model=256 (proven in Finding #359) and at d_model=512 (no
architectural constraint violated).

QED.

**Corollary 1.1 (n* at T=1000).**
n* = T / 0.8 = 1000 / 0.8 = 1250. At n=2000 (n_train=1600): T/n_train = 0.625.
No sample is visited more than once. Structural guarantee satisfied at d_model=512.

### Theorem 2 (Train-Val Gap Bound Under GL Early Stopping)

**Theorem 2.** Under GL early stopping with threshold α=5.0, the validation loss at
stopping point T* satisfies:

    val_loss(T*) ≤ (1 + α/100) × best_val_loss = 1.05 × best_val_loss

*Proof.*
Follows directly from the stopping condition:
    GL(T*) ≤ α ⟹ 100 × (val_loss(T*)/best_val_loss - 1) ≤ α
    ⟹ val_loss(T*) ≤ (1 + α/100) × best_val_loss = 1.05 × best_val_loss

This is a TIGHT bound — it holds by construction of the GL criterion, independent
of d_model.

QED.

**Connection to K883 threshold:**
At d=256 (Finding #359), measured train-val gap at n=2000 = 0.337 nats.
At d=512, the B-matrix targets are 2× larger, making the learning task harder.
We allow 2× the measured micro gap: 2 × 0.337 = 0.674 nats → K883 threshold = 0.7 nats.

This is a CONSERVATIVE threshold derived from the micro measurement × 2, not
an arbitrary value.

### Scaling Heuristic (Quality Floor at d=512 — Engineering Estimate, NOT a Theorem)

**Estimate (not a theorem).** Bartlett et al. (arXiv:1906.11300) prove benign overfitting
for *linear regression* with isotropic sub-Gaussian features. Applying their framework to
the M2P — a 2-layer transformer with nonlinear projections — violates the preconditions.
The following calculation is therefore an ENGINEERING ESTIMATE, not a bound:

    quality_ratio(d=512) ≈ quality_ratio(d=256) × (n/d_eff_512) / (n/d_eff_256)
                          ≈ 0.976 × (2000/2048) / (2000/1024)
                          ≈ 0.976 × (0.977) / (1.953)
                          ≈ 0.976 × 0.5 = 0.488

This estimate is very loose: (a) Bartlett's theorem requires linear regression, not
transformers; (b) d_eff here counts output parameters, not the effective spectral
dimensionality of the feature covariance (as Bartlett's framework requires). The
estimate of 0.488 cannot distinguish K882 (≥85%) from K884 (<60%) and has zero
predictive power for the kill criteria thresholds.

**Practical lower bound:** The K882 threshold of 85% is derived empirically by reasoning:
- d=512 makes the B-matrix targets 2× larger
- The n_train≥T guarantee still holds (Theorem 1)
- GL early stopping still prevents runaway overfitting (Theorem 2)
- The M2P architecture (d_M2P=64, L=2) has been shown sufficient at d=256 (Findings #355, #357)
- At 2× target dimensionality, some quality degradation is expected, but not catastrophic
- Conservative floor: 85% (allowing ~12pp below micro's 97.6%)
- K884 kill floor: 60% (would indicate fundamental capacity bottleneck)

The gap between 85% and 60% distinguishes two hypotheses:
- 85-97.6%: M2P scales gracefully with d_model (capacity is adequate)
- 60-85%: Degradation worse than expected (likely capacity bottleneck at d_M2P=64)
- <60%: Definitive capacity failure (must increase d_M2P or output head design)

## F. Worked Example (d=512, T=1000, n=2000)

**n_train≥T check:**
    n = 2000, train_frac = 0.8 → n_train = 1600
    T = 1000
    epochs = T / n_train = 1000 / 1600 = 0.625 epochs < 1
    → No sample is visited more than once.
    → Structural guarantee satisfied. (Theorem 1, QED)

**Output head dimensions at d=512:**
    MODULE_OUT_DIMS = [512, 512, 512, 512, 4×512] = [512, 512, 512, 512, 2048]
    For fc1 (mi=4): out_head projects d_M2P=64 → N_LAYERS × LORA_RANK × d_out
                  = 2 × 4 × 2048 = 16,384 parameters
    For wq (mi=0): out_head projects d_M2P=64 → 2 × 4 × 512 = 4,096 parameters

    Total B-matrix parameters: 2 × 4 × (512×4 + 512×4 + 512×4 + 512×4 + 2048×4)
                              = 2 × 4 × (2048 + 2048 + 2048 + 2048 + 8192)
                              = 2 × 4 × 16,384
                              = 131,072 B-matrix parameters per domain

**GL criterion example at d=512:**
    Suppose val_loss history at steps 50, 100, ..., 300: [4.2, 3.8, 3.4, 3.2, 3.3, 3.5]
    best_val_loss = 3.2 at step 200
    val_loss at step 300 = 3.5
    GL(300) = 100 × (3.5 / 3.2 - 1) = 100 × 0.0938 = 9.38 > GL_THRESHOLD=5.0
    → consecutive_gl_exceeded increments
    → After PATIENCE=5 consecutive checks: early stopping triggered

**Quality ratio computation:**
    base_loss = 4.5 (pre-SFT base model on domain val set)
    sft_loss  = 3.0 (SFT LoRA adapter quality)
    m2p_loss  = 3.2 (M2P-predicted adapter quality)
    gap = base_loss - sft_loss = 4.5 - 3.0 = 1.5 (> PARITY_GUARD_THRESHOLD=0.05)
    quality_ratio = (base_loss - m2p_loss) / gap = (4.5 - 3.2) / 1.5 = 1.3 / 1.5 = 86.7%
    → K882 PASS (86.7% ≥ 85%)
    → K884 NOT triggered (86.7% ≥ 60%)

## Self-Test (MANDATORY)

**1. What is the ONE mathematical property that makes the failure mode (cyclic overfitting) impossible?**
When n_train ≥ T (T/n_train = 0.625 at n=2000, T=1000), each training sample is seen
at most once. The gradient estimator satisfies the Ghadimi-Lan i.i.d. condition,
making cyclic memorization structurally impossible. This holds regardless of d_model.

**2. Which existing theorem(s) does the proof build on?**
- Ghadimi & Lan (2013, arXiv:1309.5549, Theorem 2.1): O(1/T) convergence — requires
  unbiased i.i.d. gradient estimates (proven d_model-independent)
- Bartlett et al. (2020, arXiv:1906.11300): benign overfitting requires n >> d_eff
  (for linear regression; applied here as a loose engineering estimate only —
  preconditions not satisfied for nonlinear transformers)
- Prechelt (1998): GL criterion guarantees val_loss(T*) ≤ 1.05 × best_val_loss
- Hardt et al. (2016, "Train Faster, Generalize Better"): generalization gap ≤ O(T/n)
  for single-pass SGD

**3. What specific numbers does the proof predict?**
- T/n_train = 1000/1600 = 0.625 (structural guarantee satisfied — no cycling)
- train-val gap at n=2000, d=512 < 0.7 nats (K883, derived as 2× micro measurement)
- quality_ratio(n=2000, d=512) ≥ 85% of SFT (K882, conservative capacity floor)
- K884 kill: quality_ratio < 60% (definitive capacity bottleneck)
- Micro reference: quality_ratio(n=2000, d=256) = 97.6% (Finding #359)

**4. What would FALSIFY the proof (not just the experiment)?**
- Theorem 1 is falsified if: train-val gap at n=2000 > 0.7 nats AND n_train=1600 < T=1000
  is NOT the cause (i.e., cycling is confirmed but gap is still ≥ 0.7 nats). This would
  indicate the M2P is memorizing through a mechanism other than gradient cycling.
- Theorem 2 is falsified if: GL criterion fails to bound val_loss ≤ 1.05 × best_val_loss
  (this cannot happen by construction; would indicate a software bug).
- K882 failure: quality_ratio < 85% means the M2P capacity at d_M2P=64 is insufficient
  for d=512 targets. This does NOT falsify Theorem 1 (which is about overfitting, not
  capacity). It would indicate that d_M2P needs to scale with d_model.
- K884 trigger: quality_ratio < 60% is a definitive capacity failure. This falsifies
  the assumption that the d_model=256 recipe transfers to d=512.

**5. How many hyperparameters does this approach add?**
Count: 0 new hyperparameters relative to exp_m2p_data_scale.
- N_SAMPLES = [1000, 2000]: the sweep variable (treatment), derived from n* = 1250
