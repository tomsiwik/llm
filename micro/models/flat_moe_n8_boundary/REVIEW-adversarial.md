### What holds

1. **Composition gap formula is correct.** `(comp_loss - joint_loss) / joint_loss * 100%` implemented correctly.

2. **Jaccard decomposition logic is correct.** The `decompose_n_domain` function properly extracts per-domain capsule slices and computes set-level Jaccard.

3. **Death rate prediction is sound.** MATH.md predicts ~93.75% from `1 - d/P_comp`. Observed: 92.5% (results.json). Good agreement.

4. **Calibration protocol is reasonable.** Same protocol applied consistently across N=2, N=5, N=8 with linearly scaled calibration steps. The ad hoc nature is acknowledged.

5. **The removal of the vacuous bound (Fix 2) is correct.** The paper now honestly states that no analytical bound is available, which is better than a vacuous one.

### What does not hold

6. **The "N>5-specific" attribution claim is falsified by results.json.** The actual N=2 post-calibration Jaccard is 0.588, which is below the 0.60 threshold. This means the Jaccard kill is triggered at ALL tested N values, not just N>5. The original REVISE review's concern -- that the kill might be a universal calibration artifact -- was correct, and the revision's "fix" actually confirms this rather than refuting it. The paper claims the opposite because it uses incorrect numbers.

7. **The "gap is flat at ~+6%" claim is approximately supported but with caveats.** results.json shows N=2: +6.13%, N=5: +6.04%, N=8: +7.39%. The N=8 gap is higher than the other two, and seed 42 at N=8 reaches 10.26%. With std=2.49% and only 3 seeds, the 10% threshold is within roughly 1 standard deviation of the mean. The gap PASS is more marginal than PAPER.md presents.

## Experimental Design

### Does the experiment test the stated hypothesis?

Yes. The code is well-structured, the protocol is consistent across N, and the controls (joint training, zero-shot, single-domain specialist) are appropriate. The experimental design is good; the problem is in the reporting.

### Controls

- Joint training baseline: properly controlled with N*200 total steps and N*P capsules
- Zero-shot baseline: confirms calibration is necessary (gap explodes without it)
- Cross-N comparison: same protocol, same seeds -- this is the key improvement from the revision

### The domain confound

PAPER.md Limitation 6 correctly notes that different N values use different domain splits (binary for N=2, quintary for N=5, octonary for N=8). These are not nested subsets, so cross-N comparisons have a confound from domain boundary effects. This is acceptable at micro scale but should be flagged for macro.

1. **P/d ratio at macro is 2.7 per domain vs 2 at micro.** At N=8, macro would have P/d=21.5, still overcomplete but the death rate would be determined by the actual weight distribution, not the P/d ratio alone. The micro prediction of 93% death may not transfer.

More importantly, the actual results.json data tells a different story than the paper claims: the Jaccard kill is universal (N=2 also fails), not N>5-specific. The paper must be rewritten to match the actual data.
